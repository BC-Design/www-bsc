Brandon and I have looked at this a reasonable amount, and there's really no killer app out there right now. My personal software of choice for literature management is Zotero, which integrates best with Firefox, but also connects easily with Chrome and Safari via an extra standalone app. This is really useful for me because it makes the step of "marking a paper for future reference in an organized fashion" a super simple process (connectors for most major publisher websites, arXiv, metadata by DOI or ISBN, etc.), but its downfall is that it doesn't support mobile out of the box, and doesn't support electronic annotation.

bgillespie
2015-10-02 18:08
My strategy is to use Zotero for general archival / reference purposes, and to print out and sort papers that I'm interacting with heavily and want to annotate.

bgillespie

Also, its support for mobile was likewise crap in the past. I also don't like the fact that it was purchased by publishing titan Elsevier a few years ago. Perhaps Brandon has more to say about the current state of affairs, since I haven't interacted with it for a year or two at least.
