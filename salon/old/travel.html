mbyron
2016-04-25 18:27
Thanks for the tips, y'all! If you ever get the chance to go to Mojave, I'd take it. Beautiful, desolate, diverse, and procrastinator-friendly (no entrance fee, free roadside camping). Kind of a hybrid between car camping and backpacking-- if you see a turnout with a campfire ring, you're allowed to camp there (most roads are dirt/gravel-- we did it with a Prius and would have had more access with a high-clearance/4WD vehicle). We had no trouble finding spots despite it apparently being peak season. I actually wouldn't recommend backpacking in MNP because it is so large-- if you want to see all the distinct regions (dunes, joshua tree forest, volcanic canyons, etc) you'll need to drive the 30+ miles between each part of the preserve. Plus carrying water for more than 2 days kind of sucks unless you have a car.

I've never heard anyone call South Congress "SOCO." Chuy's is a chain... it's not bad, but not exactly Austin-specific Tex-Mex. Silly veggie-heads will be missing out on good steak and barbecue places. Burger places usually have a black bean option, so you can avail yourself of landmarks like Hop Doddy or Doc's.

seekarete
2016-06-08 23:02
SXSW is the big one, but it seems like there's a festival of some kind every other weekend, so it pays to plan ahead. The river is very popular for kayaks and stand up paddle boards which can be rented a few different places. Bat-watching is a thing, too, though I've never done it. The Circuit of the Americas is just out of town if you have a desire to see MotoGP or Formula 1 racing. They also hold large concerts there.
