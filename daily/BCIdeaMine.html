== find_all

You can filter multiple attributes at once by passing in more than one keyword argument

        soup.find_all(href=re.compile("elsie"), id='link1')

Find tags beneath other tags:

        soup.select("body a")

        soup.select("html head title")

from string import strip

import re

soup = BeautifulSoup(open("participants.html"))

"Radio frequency shielding techniques are quite different from those intended for quasi-static electric fields. For example,

an automobile provides its occupants with a measure of lightning protection, but does little to shield RF emissions. For electromagnetic radiation, any tiny hole in the enclosure will act as an aperture through which a small amount of radiation will escape. Walls made from solid materials are the most effective, but are very costly to manufacture."

= Overview

A ticket-based system for keeping track of goals and progress!

This could be incredibly useful for research, personal projects... even apartment maintenance!

Software development has already demonstrated that ticket-based systems are highly scalable.

I'm sure there are already tons of off-the-shelf systems that I could adopt or use as models to build my own!

Combines the 'to-do list' and 'task log' concepts, allowing you to keep track of what you want to do, what you wanted to do, and what you actually did!

Intentional Communities

I talked with Zach tonight about what, exactly, we want to contribute to the world - as I put it, “what service do we want to provide to the world?”.  Zach suggested that going home and reading and dancing and rock climbing, from a consequentialist perspective, is no different from going high and getting wasted and doing nothing. We need to have a “magnum opus” that we are striving for.

I thought of two big projects that would be suitable lifetime-scale goals: one, start a consulting company; two, advance the state-of-the-art in the theoretical understanding and practical implementation of intentional communities.

I described how we could get a bunch of talent together and provide consulting services. We could steal clients from previous consulting jobs, and/or work below market rate to build up our reputation for quality work. You really need just one person to bring in clients to start: they can tap the talent they need, and the company can grow organically as the people later in the client management pipeline take on more responsibility.  It could make a fantastic part time gig! (Zach pointed out that he already makes an insane rate, so it would be hard for him to do better)

I talked about my renewed interest in community due to my good experience living with Jacob. It CAN work if the personalities match right! Zach reiterated how important it is for everyone in the community to feel strongly connected with everyone wise “so they would always know who they were screwing”. We talked about the Hutterites and how this is built right into their society (~220 people = Someone's Number).  I think it's important for people to have their own space, and Zach opined that sharing a wall is always less good than a village-with-common-spaces configuration; I thought about that, and it is true that most societies were physically organized that way! Zach thought of could be cool to try several models in several places simultaneously, to explore the S and iterate.

So let's build some kind of intentional community.
