TICKET-BASED SYSTEM

= Overview

A ticket-based system for keeping track of goals and progress!

This could be incredibly useful for research, personal projects... even apartment maintenance!

Software development has already demonstrated that ticket-based systems are highly scalable.

I'm sure there are already tons of off-the-shelf systems that I could adopt or use as models to build my own!

Why not plan your projects and your life like a software project?  Decide what 'features' the next release will have, open 'feature request' and 'bug fix' tickets, and you're on your way!

Combines the 'to-do list' and 'task log' concepts, allowing you to keep track of what you want to do, what you wanted to do, and what you actually did!

= Fields in a Ticket

Ticket Name

Ticket Number

Ticket Description

Current Status

Date Opened, Closed

Opener, Assignee

List of Tasks

List of Products

List of Links and References

Dated status updates, notes, and discussion

All tickets are listed and linked to from a spreadsheet. The ticket documents contain the above fields.

= Related Ideas

Tickets that automatically 'renew' after a set time

        would apply well for maintenance tasks: cleaning, financial updates, etc

Automatic ticket creation with a simple web-accessible form

        automatically populates many fields; adds ticket to spreadsheet, generates ticket document from template

=== TICKET-BASED SYSTEM

= Overview

A ticket-based system for keeping track of goals and progress!

This could be incredibly useful for research, personal projects... even apartment maintenance!

Software development has already demonstrated that ticket-based systems are highly scalable.

I'm sure there are already tons of off-the-shelf systems that I could adopt or use as models to build my own!

Why not plan your projects and your life like a software project?  Decide what 'features' the next release will have, open 'feature request' and 'bug fix' tickets, and you're on your way!

Combines the 'to-do list' and 'task log' concepts, allowing you to keep track of what you want to do, what you wanted to do, and what you actually did!

= Fields in a Ticket

Ticket Name

Ticket Number

Ticket Description

Current Status

Date Opened, Closed

Opener, Assignee

List of Tasks

List of Products

List of Links and References

Dated status updates, notes, and discussion

All tickets are listed and linked to from a spreadsheet. The ticket documents contain the above fields.

Tickets that automatically 'renew' after a set time

        would apply well for maintenance tasks: cleaning, financial updates, etc

Automatic ticket creation with a simple web-accessible form

        automatically populates many fields; adds ticket to spreadsheet, generates ticket document from template
