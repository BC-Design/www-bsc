The interviews don't do @mrmoneymustache justice, but his site is funny and a good resource.  The article pitch is always "look how early he retired!", but his philosophy is more about rational and analytical consideration of what time and money can buy and how we choose to use the resources we have.  Pete has inspired me (and inspired me to inspire others) to think more carefully about how I spend money and how that aligns with my larger goal of making the world a better place.

It's a fact that well-positioned graduates in high-demand fields are climbing into the six figures before they're climbing into their 30's.  People making that kind of money have always existed, and I'd rather they temper consumption and build an invested fortune than the big screen-sportscar-McMansion alternative.

I'll be stepping into a similar economic environment in a few short months.  Thanks to Pete, I realize that I prioritize the freedom to innovate and explore more than I care about living comfortably.  Instead of spending everything I make and saving some obligatory leftovers, I have a vision of substantially scaled-back lifestyle that will support a future of entrepreneurial ventures.
