Not to disparage the moderation efforts—some moderation is great, just to keep things on the rails—but I

agree with @therdash and @eugene that a light touch is the most productive approach.  A project in this early stage needs all of the interest and participation it can get, and one negatively-perceived interaction with The Authorities is all it takes to turn someone off to participating in a community.

Slack is, by design, a great chatroom and a miserable database.  Slack doesn't support channel threading, so conversations on individual topics have no clear starting point and scrolling up to figure out what's going on is a non-starter.  In a no-cost moderate-volume Slack like this one, the entire archive older than two weeks is falling off the end of the world anyway.  I suspect people are coming here to ask questions, and a few are sticking around to join ongoing conversations that they're interested in, but I seriously doubt anyone is trying to use this like a wiki.
